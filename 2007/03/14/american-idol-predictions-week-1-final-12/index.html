American Idol Predictions: Week 1 Final 12

March 14, 2007 By Gitr 8 Comments

Last night’s show was pretty good. There was some fantastic singing, good integration with the theme going along with Diana Ross being their mentor for the week, and the back and forth between Ryan and Simon during Melinda’s Q&A was priceless. For those of you who missed the exchange, it went down a road of Simon saying Ryan wears high heels, Ryan saying that Simon should stay out of his closet, Simon saying he’s waiting for Ryan to come out of it, and Ryan saying this has nothing to do with his shoes, but Melinda’s next song…segway complete. Each comment got more and more “OMG! Did he just say that on live TV?” Priceless.

Last night’s recap:

Brandon Rogers:
He was weak and forgot his words. The singing was fine, but the performance lacked.

Melinda Doolittle:
WOW!!! What a performer. She sang “Home” and brought the house down. Paula was crying and Melinda looked like she had just had the natural “in the zone” performance of a lifetime.

Chris Sligh:
No doubt this guy can sing. My woes about Sundance are officially over. Chris would have beaten him in the end. I didn’t recognize the song and thought it was terrific, but the judges said they didn’t like his remix of a classic. It didn’t bother me one big because I didn’t have anything to compare it to.

Gina Glocksen:
She sang “Love Child” and got a bit sqeaky in the high parts, and Simon said “it was forgettable.” To me, she didn’t do too hot, but she was still ahead of Brandon for the night.

Sanjaya Malakar:
I’m starting to sound like a broken record here. He sucks. The boy came out with “Ain’t No Mountain High Enough” and never got louder than the instruments. He’s afraid to pull out the stops. He’s like a hotel bar singer. He got the best quotes of the night from the judges. Randy: “What’s going down, dawg?” and Simon: “I’ll say something positive. How old are you? [Sanjaya answers “17”]… You’re brave.”

Haley Scarnato:
She did pretty good, although she started quiet and came out in the end. She got her first compliment from Simon. Ever. If I remember correctly, she got to Hollywood on 2 votes, without Simon’s approval.

Phil Stacey:
I agree with everything Randy said: “Boring, but great vocals.” Although, I’ve noticed that he doesn’t have a low range at all. I am sure LaKisha can sing lower.

LaKisha Jones:
She sang “God Bless The Child” after never having heard the songs or read the lyrics before. I went, “Uh oh. She’s going to forget the words.” Man, she didn’t, and she got right on up there with Melinda. Fantasic.

Blake Lewis:
He updated “You Keep Me Hanging On” in a very cool way, but the judges told him to not mess with a classic. I thought it was really great.

Stephanie Edwards:
What I noted about her performance was that her vocals were dead on, but her song didn’t connect with me, or really even with her. It was just a song.

Chris Richardson:
To quote myself, “Ugh! Dawg!” Paula liked him, proving she is on crack or something. If she was at home, I’d swear she had the volume down. Simon was spot-on and called it “dreadful.”

Jordin Sparks:
She came out and laid it all out on the line. The best part is that she “Made it a three-girl race,” according to Randy. I agree.

There is your recap, and now for the toughest part. I’d really like to see Sanjaya go because he didn’t bring it. There was no yo, no volume, and no desire. But the 15-year-old girls will still vote for him. Brandon immediately started at the bottom by forgetting the words, but I think Chris Richardson’s performance was far worse and will get the boot.

Final answer: Chris Richardson.
