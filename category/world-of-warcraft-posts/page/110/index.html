Getting geared up

May 22, 2006 By Gitr Leave a Comment

Gitr’s had a whirlwind weekend of new gear.

First, a public service message:

Do not play with a lot of money in had while tired early in the morning. In the AH, prices are sorted by the bid price. I was looking at the prices for Heart of Fire after getting the Thorium Brotherhood’s transmute recipe, and found some for 1g. Just above that, I saw one for 99. I assumed that it was 99s because it was above the others, and didn’t pay attention to the confirmation box, either. All of a sudden I was left with 13g in my bags when I had just opened all my successful auction mails to give me over 100g! DOH! Since I’d bought 3 things in a row, I ran to the mail with a sneaky suspicion that I paid 100g for a 1g item. I did. DON’T DO THAT!

Now, back to our regularly scheduled program. Check out Gitr’s profile to see why he’s so happy with his gear. It’s amazing what a really good Baron run will get you. To summarize his current Valor/Heroism pieces:

Spaulders of Valor
Bracers of Heroism
Gauntlets of Heroism
Belt of Heroism
Legplates of Valor
Boots of Valor

Baron also dropped me a nice helm, I already had his sword and cape.

Helm of the Executioner
Cape of the Black Baron
Skullforge Reaver

Looking at my gear, it looks life Baron runs have been quite good to me. Those items, Band of Flesh, Bone Slicing Hatchet, and a Seal of the Dawn have a very positive impact on my damage.

Saturday afternoon, I got to do a Scholo run and got a shield upgrade from the ghoul boss: Rattlecage Buckler.

It was sometime on Saturday that I realized a nice money-making scheme that involves a little travel and an item used in the Dungeon set upgrade quests. I’ll let you guys figure out what item it is that, if fetched, can bring in lots of money. I already started making Goblin Rocket Fuel by the bunches because they sell for about twice more than the cost of mats. On a side note, I sold all 12 Arcanite Bars that I had for my Lionheart helm to fund my dungeon set upgrades. It was sad to see all that money go, but I sure have a lot of nice gear now, and Grimr can keep making them with some Gitr crafting sprinkled in. I have all the other mats for it for when I’m ready.

I foresee many UBRS and Scholo runs until I get the Valor helm and breastplate. Tonight or tomorrow I’ll start working on the quest to start the banner to convert my next 3 pieces, which I gladly already have.

Casual Raiders has Forums

May 11, 2006 By Gitr Leave a Comment

It’s been a while since I put it up, but since no one is coming and joining Casual Raiders in droves, I’ll put it up here. If you have a 55-60 toon that wants to raid, but doesn’t have a set schedule for playing or your schedule conflicts with your current guild, go ahead and join the forums and let others know when you’ll be available.

I see the guild starting from the forums side first, and then as more people join and match up times to play, more people will join the guild.

Casual Raiders

Don’t forget, this will be a mostly un-maintained guild that will eventually have officers and leadership, but is for now going to be kept to letting people do more organized in-house “PuGs,” more or less.
