Ding 30, 31; Starting a Tankadin Run

October 1, 2007 By Gitr 16 Comments

It was quite a weekend with Paladr, as we were determined to hit level 30 to become a true tankadin. That moment has finally arrived, as I’m now able to take on multiple mobs in a fashion I’ve never done before, although, carefully. we remembered reading that the AOE tanking should be done with melee beasts and other mobs that don’t flee. That really can screw things up, in a number of ways, which we will outline below.

Please keep in mind, at the time of this writing, Paladr is only level 31, with most of the stuff following being from his time at level 30 and does not have all the “cool” tools for tanking as a pally. What he does have is awesome so far, and is bringing us a lot of enjoyment.

Things that I have learned from my mistakes already:

  • You will run out of mana if mobs keep fleeing and bringing help.
  • It is better to hit fast than hard.
  • Your aura is crucial.

Master Pyreanor, the Paladin Trainer in Orgrimmar, Kalimdor

September 28, 2007 By Gitr 2 Comments

Master Pyreanor is the Paladin Trainer in Orgrimmar. He stands just to Thrall’s left side, off the raised throne, on your right entering his back chamber. Yes, folks, it appears that all the sources (and there are a lot of them, even on the official forums, although WoWWiki is reflecting a correct list) about the only Horde paladin trainers being in UC and SM are dead wrong. Here he is, in the flesh. He even trains you if you pay him some gold.

No longer will there be hundreds of Horde paladins taking the long swim across the big pond to train every 2-6 levels, depending on how anal you are.

Buttered Monkeys Are Now Recruiting

September 27, 2007 By Gitr Leave a Comment

We are opening up applications to our fine Horde PvE guild of casual to semi-hardcore players. You will get the most grouping attention with a fast-leveling new player or a 30-something or a 60+. We currently do not have any level 70s, although some will be transferring at a later date.

Faction: Horde
Realm: Velen – US
Time Zone: Pacific (-8 UTC)
Classes Open: Priest, Warrior, Mage, Shaman, Rogue, Druid

What to expect:

  • Fun – We play because it’s a game.
  • Help – Some quests just can’t be done solo. Don’t bug people to death for help or with questions readily.
  • Casual raiding – This will all depend on how many members we have and of what type. Most everyone can spare an hour to do the Outland 5-mans every night of the week.
  • Maturity – We aren’t kids and lead busy adult lives. To my knowledge, we are all college to middle-aged and married. If you can’t play every night because of family/work that’s fine. Our only rule regarding that is to try to let someone know you can’t make it if there is something planned and you signed up for it. Simple.

Our application process is easy and painless. Go to https://gitrknowswow.com/butteredmonkeysforum to register and apply for membership. If you make sense and can follow directions, you are almost certain to have fun being a Buttered Monkey.
